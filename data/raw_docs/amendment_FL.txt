**NEXUS INSURANCE COMPANY**
**FLORIDA AMENDATORY ENDORSEMENT**
**FORM NX-FL-AMD**

This endorsement amends the policy to which it is attached, issued by Nexus Insurance Company, a Florida corporation, and is made a part thereof. All terms, conditions, and exclusions otherwise applicable to the policy remain unchanged except as specifically modified herein.

**SECTION I – PROPERTY COVERAGES**

**1. PERILS INSURED AGAINST – SECTION I – EXCLUSIONS**

a. **Hurricane Deductible.** Notwithstanding any contrary provision in the policy, the following deductible shall apply to loss or damage caused by a Hurricane, as defined in Section 627.4025(2)(a), Florida Statutes:

   i. A separate deductible of two percent (2%) of Coverage A – Dwelling Limit shall apply to any loss or damage caused by a Hurricane during the period in which the Hurricane is classified as such by the National Hurricane Center of the National Weather Service.

   ii. This deductible is in addition to any other deductible provided for in the policy and shall be the only deductible applicable to Hurricane losses.

   iii. The deductible shall be applied once per Hurricane event, regardless of the number of occurrences or claims made under the policy.

**2. ADDITIONAL COVERAGES – SECTION I**

a. **Sinkhole Losses.**

   i. **Basic Sinkhole Coverage.** This policy provides coverage for sinkhole losses as defined in Section 627.706, Florida Statutes, subject to the terms, conditions, and exclusions of the policy.

   ii. **Catastrophic Ground Cover Collapse.** Loss resulting from Catastrophic Ground Cover Collapse, as defined in Section 627.706(2)(c), Florida Statutes, is covered under this policy. Such collapse must be of a type described in Section 627.706(2)(d), Florida Statutes, and must be the direct result of underground soil erosion or the removal of subsurface earth.

   iii. **Exclusions.** No coverage is provided for sinkhole losses that do not result in a Catastrophic Ground Cover Collapse, including damage caused by gradual subsidence, settlement, or expansion of soil.

**SECTION II – LIABILITY COVERAGES**

No amendments are made to Section II – Liability Coverages by this endorsement.

**SECTION I – CONDITIONS**

**3. YOUR DUTIES AFTER LOSS**

a. **Hurricane Losses – Reporting Requirement.**

   i. In the event of loss or damage caused by a Hurricane, as defined in Section 627.4025(2)(a), Florida Statutes, the insured shall provide written notice of such loss to Nexus Insurance Company within ninety (90) days of the date of loss.

   ii. Failure to provide such notice within the prescribed period shall not invalidate the claim unless Nexus Insurance Company is prejudiced thereby.

   iii. The insured shall otherwise comply with all other duties imposed by the policy and this endorsement following a Hurricane loss.

**4. APPRAISAL**

No amendments are made to the Appraisal provision by this endorsement.

**GENERAL PROVISIONS**

**5. CITIZENS PROPERTY INSURANCE CORPORATION DISCLOSURE**

a. **Notice.** The policy to which this endorsement is attached may be considered surplus lines insurance under Section 626.914, Florida Statutes. If the insured’s property is located in an area where coverage is available from Citizens Property Insurance Corporation, the insured is hereby notified that such coverage may be available at a lower cost.

b. **Comparison.** The insured is encouraged to compare the coverage, terms, and premiums of this policy with those offered by Citizens Property Insurance Corporation to determine the most suitable coverage for their needs.

**6. SEVERABILITY**

If any provision of this endorsement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall remain in full force and effect.

**7. ENTIRE CONTRACT**

This endorsement, together with the policy to which it is attached, constitutes the entire contract between the insured and Nexus Insurance Company.

**ADOPTION OF AMENDMENTS**

Nexus Insurance Company adopts the above amendments effective as of the inception or renewal date of the policy to which this endorsement is attached.
