**NEXUS INSURANCE COMPANY**
**COMMERCIAL PROPERTY INSURANCE POLICY**
**POLICY FORM NX-CP-1**

---

### **AGREEMENT**

1. **Insuring Agreement.**
   a. **Property Coverage.** In consideration of the premium and subject to all the terms, conditions, exclusions, and limitations herein, Nexus Insurance Company ("We," "Us," or "Our") agrees to pay for direct physical loss of or damage to Covered Property at the premises described in the Declarations, caused by or resulting from any Covered Cause of Loss, subject to the terms, conditions, and exclusions of this Policy.
   b. **Business Interruption Coverage.** We also agree to pay for the actual loss of Business Income and/or Extra Expense sustained by the Insured during the "Period of Restoration" as a result of direct physical loss of or damage to Covered Property at the described premises caused by or resulting from any Covered Cause of Loss, subject to the terms, conditions, and exclusions of this Policy.

---

### **DEFINITIONS**

1. **Accumulation Period.** The period of time, as specified in the Declarations, during which the suspension of Business Income must occur in order to trigger coverage under Section II.
2. **Business Income.** Net income (net profit or loss before income taxes) that would have been earned or incurred, and continuing normal operating expenses incurred, including payroll, during the "Period of Restoration."
3. **Covered Cause of Loss.** A "Risk of Direct Physical Loss" unless the loss is excluded or limited in Section III.
4. **Covered Property.** The property described in the Declarations, including:
   a. Buildings and structures at the described premises;
   b. Business Personal Property owned by the Insured and located at the described premises;
   c. Personal Property of Others in the care, custody, or control of the Insured, if so indicated in the Declarations.
5. **Extra Expense.** Expenses incurred during the "Period of Restoration" that are over and above the normal operating expenses and that would not have been incurred if there had been no direct physical loss or damage to Covered Property.
6. **Period of Restoration.** The period of time that:
   a. Begins with the date of direct physical loss or damage caused by or resulting from any Covered Cause of Loss at the described premises; and
   b. Ends on the earlier of:
      (1) The date when the property should be repaired, rebuilt, or replaced with reasonable speed and similar quality; or
      (2) The date when Business Income would be restored if no extraordinary efforts were made to accelerate the rebuilding or repair.
7. **Risk of Direct Physical Loss.** A fortuitous event resulting in direct physical loss or damage to Covered Property, unless excluded or limited in Section III.

---

### **SECTION I – BUILDING AND PERSONAL PROPERTY COVERAGE**

1. **Property Covered.**
   a. **Buildings.** We cover buildings and structures at the described premises, including:
      (1) Completed additions;
      (2) Fixtures, including outdoor fixtures;
      (3) Permanently installed machinery and equipment;
      (4) Personal Property owned by the Insured and used to service the buildings or structures.
   b. **Business Personal Property.** We cover business personal property owned by the Insured and located at the described premises, including:
      (1) Furniture and fixtures;
      (2) Machinery and equipment;
      (3) Stock;
      (4) All other personal property owned by the Insured and used in the business.
   c. **Personal Property of Others.** We cover personal property of others in the care, custody, or control of the Insured, if so indicated in the Declarations.

2. **Property Not Covered.**
   a. **Accounts, Bills, Currency, Food Stamps, or Other Evidence of Debt, Money, or Securities.** We do not cover these items unless otherwise provided in writing.
   b. **Animals, unless owned by others and their care is your business.**
   c. **Automobiles held for sale.**
   d. **Bridges, roadways, walks, patios, or other paved surfaces.**
   e. **Contraband, or property in the course of illegal transportation or trade.**
   f. **Cost of excavations, grading, or backfilling.**
   g. **Foundations of buildings, structures, machinery, or boilers if their foundations are below the lowest basement floor or the surface of the ground if there is no basement.**
   h. **Land, water, growing crops, or lawns.**
   i. **Personal property while airborne or waterborne.**
   j. **Retaining walls not a part of a building.**
   k. **Underground pipes, flues, or drains.**

3. **Covered Causes of Loss.**
   a. **Special Form.** Covered Causes of Loss means "Risk of Direct Physical Loss" unless the loss is excluded or limited in Section III.

4. **Additional Coverages.**
   a. **Debris Removal.** We will pay the expense of removing debris of Covered Property caused by or resulting from a Covered Cause of Loss during the policy period. This expense is included in the limit of insurance for the damaged property.
   b. **Preservation of Property.** We will pay for loss or damage to Covered Property moved to protect it from further damage, for up to 30 days at the new location.
   c. **Fire Department Service Charge.** We will pay up to $1,000 for fire department service charges incurred when the fire department is called to save or protect Covered Property from a Covered Cause of Loss.

5. **Limits of Insurance.**
   a. The limits of insurance are stated in the Declarations.
   b. The most we will pay for loss or damage in any one occurrence is the applicable limit of insurance shown in the Declarations.

---

### **SECTION II – BUSINESS INTERRUPTION COVERAGE**

1. **Business Income Coverage.**
   a. We will pay for the actual loss of Business Income due to the necessary suspension of "operations" during the "Period of Restoration" caused by direct physical loss of or damage to Covered Property at the described premises caused by or resulting from a Covered Cause of Loss.
   b. The suspension must be caused by direct physical loss of or damage to Covered Property at the described premises.

2. **Extra Expense Coverage.**
   a. We will pay Extra Expenses incurred during the "Period of Restoration" to avoid or minimize the suspension of business operations.

3. **Loss Determination.**
   a. The amount of Business Income loss will be determined based on:
      (1) The net income of the business before the loss occurred;
      (2) The probable net income of the business if no loss had occurred;
      (3) Operating expenses, including payroll, necessary to resume operations with the same quality of service as existed prior to the loss.

4. **Period of Restoration.**
   a. The "Period of Restoration" will begin on the date of direct physical loss or damage and will end on the earlier of:
      (1) The date when the property should be repaired, rebuilt, or replaced with reasonable speed and similar quality; or
      (2) The date when Business Income would be restored if no extraordinary efforts were made to accelerate the rebuilding or repair.

---

### **SECTION III – EXCLUSIONS**

1. **General Exclusions.** We will not pay for loss or damage caused directly or indirectly by any of the following:
   a. **Ordinance or Law.** Loss resulting from the enforcement of any ordinance or law regulating the construction, repair, or demolition of buildings or structures.
   b. **Earth Movement.** Loss caused by or resulting from earth movement, including earthquake, landslide, mudslide, or sinkhole.
   c. **Governmental Action.** Loss caused by or resulting from the destruction, confiscation, or seizure of Covered Property by order of any governmental or public authority.
   d. **Nuclear Hazard.** Loss caused by or resulting from nuclear reaction, radiation, or radioactive contamination.
   e. **Utility Services.** Loss caused by or resulting from the failure of power, heat, refrigeration supply, or communication services supplied to the described premises, unless the failure is caused by a Covered Cause of Loss that occurs on the described premises.
   f. **War and Military Action.** Loss caused by or resulting from war, including undeclared war, civil war, insurrection, rebellion, or revolution.

2. **Additional Exclusions.**
   a. **Vacancy.** If the building where loss or damage occurs has been vacant for more than 60 consecutive days before the loss or damage, we will not pay for any loss or damage caused by:
      (1) Vandalism;
      (2) Sprinkler leakage, unless the sprinkler system was protected against freezing;
      (3) Breakage of building glass;
      (4) Water damage;
      (5) Theft or attempted theft.
   b. **Neglect.** Loss caused by or resulting from the Insured’s neglect to reasonably protect Covered Property from further damage.
   c. **Pollution.** Loss caused by or resulting from the discharge, dispersal, seepage, migration, or release of pollutants unless the discharge, dispersal, or release is itself caused by a Covered Cause of Loss.

---

### **SECTION IV – CONDITIONS**

1. **Duties in the Event of Loss.**
   a. **Notice of Loss.** The Insured must:
      (1) Notify Us as soon as practicable of any loss or damage;
      (2) Provide a description of the property damaged;
      (3) State the time, place, and circumstances of the occurrence;
      (4) Submit to examination under oath.
   b. **Police Report.** If the loss or damage is caused by theft, burglary, or robbery, the Insured must file a police report.
   c. **Protection of Property.** The Insured must:
      (1) Protect Covered Property from further damage;
      (2) Keep an accurate record of repair expenses;
      (3) Permit Us to inspect the property as often as reasonably required.

2. **Appraisal.**
   a. If the Insured and We disagree on the amount of loss, either may demand an appraisal. Each party will select a competent appraiser, and the appraisers will select an umpire. If they fail to agree, the umpire will decide the amount of loss.

3. **Other Insurance.**
   a. If other insurance applies to the same loss, We will pay only our proportion of the loss.

4. **Subrogation.**
   a. Any rights of recovery the Insured has against any person or organization for loss or damage to Covered Property will be transferred to Us to the extent of Our payment.

5. **Concealment, Misrepresentation, or Fraud.**
   a. The entire policy will be void if the Insured has concealed or misrepresented any material fact or engaged in fraudulent conduct in connection with the policy or any claim.

6. **Cancellation.**
   a. This policy may be canceled by Us or the Insured by mailing or delivering written notice to the other party. The effective date of cancellation will be:
      (1) For cancellation by Us, 10 days after notice is mailed;
      (2) For cancellation by the Insured, the date specified in the notice.

7. **Changes.**
   a. Any change to this policy must be in writing and signed by Us.

8. **Examination of Books and Records.**
   a. We may examine and audit the Insured’s books and records related to this policy at any time during the policy period and for up to three years afterward.
