**NEXUS INSURANCE COMPANY**
**FORM NX-CA-AMD**
**CALIFORNIA AMENDATORY ENDORSEMENT**

THIS ENDORSEMENT MODIFIES INSURANCE PROVIDED UNDER POLICY FORM NX-HO-3 AND IS REQUIRED BY THE STATE OF CALIFORNIA.

**Section 1. Cancellation Notice Period**
1.1. Notwithstanding any provision to the contrary in Policy Form NX-HO-3, Section I, Condition 4 (Cancellation), the minimum notice period for cancellation of this policy by the insurer shall be extended from ten (10) days to twenty (20) days, as required by California Insurance Code § 660 et seq. This amendment applies to all cancellations, whether for nonpayment of premium or otherwise.

**Section 2. Wildfire Provisions**
2.1. Coverage for Loss Caused by Wildfire Smoke. Notwithstanding any contrary provision in Policy Form NX-HO-3, Section I, Coverage A (Dwelling) and Coverage B (Other Structures), this policy is amended to include coverage for direct physical loss to the insured property caused by wildfire smoke, provided such loss is not otherwise excluded under the policy. For purposes of this endorsement, "wildfire smoke" means particulate matter resulting from a wildfire that causes damage to the insured property.
2.2. This amendment is made in accordance with California Insurance Code § 675.1 and is subject to all other terms, conditions, and exclusions of the policy.

**Section 3. Fair Claims Settlement Practices**
3.1. The insurer agrees to comply with California’s Fair Claims Settlement Practices Regulations, as set forth in California Code of Regulations, Title 10, Chapter 5, Subchapter 7.4, §§ 2695.1 et seq. This includes, but is not limited to:
   a. Prompt investigation of claims;
   b. Timely and reasonable evaluations of claims;
   c. Good faith efforts to effectuate prompt, fair, and equitable settlements of claims in which liability has become reasonably clear;
   d. Disclosure of all pertinent benefits, coverages, or other provisions of the insurance policy under which a claim is made.
3.2. Any violation of these regulations may constitute an unfair claims practice under California Insurance Code § 790.03(h).

**Section 4. Mold Disclosure Requirements**
4.1. The insurer shall comply with California’s mold disclosure requirements, as set forth in California Insurance Code § 2071. This includes:
   a. Providing written notice to the insured at the time of application and policy renewal regarding the availability of mold coverage, if any, and the limitations or exclusions thereto;
   b. Disclosing any dollar limitations or exclusions for mold claims in the policy declarations or endorsements.
4.2. Any policy exclusion or limitation relating to mold must be clearly and conspicuously stated in the policy, as required by § 2071.2.

**Section 5. Severability**
5.1. If any provision of this endorsement is held to be invalid or unenforceable, the remainder of the endorsement shall remain in full force and effect.

**Section 6. Applicable Law**
6.1. This endorsement is subject to all applicable laws and regulations of the State of California, including but not limited to the California Insurance Code and the California Code of Regulations.

**Section 7. Entire Agreement**
7.1. This endorsement, together with Policy Form NX-HO-3, constitutes the entire agreement between the insurer and the insured with respect to the matters amended herein.
